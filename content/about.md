{{< profile username="dougdomingos" >}}

Undergraduate student in Computer Science at the Federal University of Campina
Grande (UFCG).

As a pragmatic software engineer, my goal is to deliver reliable, high-value
solutions to end users, regardless of the technologies involved. I believe that
exceptional products are born from the synergy of flexible teams, clear
communication, and decisions strictly guided by solid metrics and tangible
impacts.

Behind the pragmatism, however, lies the soul of an artist. I'm fascinated by
how the experiences and creativity of a team are translated into the
problem-solving process. For me, the true beauty of software architecture lies
in the way teams extract the most out of the available resources to build
incredible user experiences.

Beyond DevOps and Infrastructure, I have a strong interest in Pixel Art and Game
Development. After all, creating games is what brought me into programming, and
this passion is still alive, more than ever. Today, I invest part of my free
time in traditional and digital art, as a way to express what can't be contained
within the boundaries of an IDE.

With plans to debut as an Indie GameDev at some point in the future, I'm
convinced that my technical background, alongside my artistic side, will merge
into a genuine identity that will be etched into every story I plan to tell.
